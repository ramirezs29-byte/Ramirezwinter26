Origin of the Game

Softball originated in Chicago in 1887 and was introduced by George Hancock.

How It Evolved Over Time

Over time, the game moved outdoors and different styles developed. Faster, more competitive play became fastpitch, while easier, recreational play became slowpitch.

Key Milestones in Softball History

  1. 1887: George Hancock invented the game of softball in Chicago on Thanksgiving Day.
  2. 1895: Two years later, Lewis Robwe Sr. in Minneapolis developed a 12-inch ball that was first called "diamond ball," which later became known as softball.
  3. 1926: The name "softball" was chosen by Walter Hankanson; it had previously been called diamond ball or indoor baseball.
  4. 1933: Softball gained national attention with the first national tournament held at the Chicago World's Fair.
  5. 1940-1950: Fastpitch became more widely played and formalized.
  6. 1952: Women's pitching distance was set to 38 feet.
  7. 1965: Australia won the inaugural Women's Softball World Championship in Melbourne, defeating the United States.
  8. 1996: Team USA won the inaugural Olympic gold medal in softball, finishing 5-0 in pool play.
  9. 2008-2021: Softball was removed from the Olympics after 2008, returned for the Tokyo Games in 2020, and is scheduled to appear again in 2028.

Primary Versions of Softball

  • Fastpitch
  • Slowpitch
  • Modified-pitch softball

My Personal Experience

I have been playing the sport for six years. I first started when I was five in T-ball. As I grew older, at age eight I played baseball (with the boys) for two years because there were few girls. I enjoyed the game and decided to start playing softball, eventually moving to a slightly competitive level called travel ball, where we sometimes play against other teams to compete for a ring or a trophy in tournaments.
